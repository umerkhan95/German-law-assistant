## TECHNICAL DECISION MATRIX

### Choose RAG Pattern Based On:

1. **Simple RAG**: Single-question research → Good enough
2. **Agentic RAG**: Complex multi-step research → Best (Harvey, CoCounsel, Westlaw)
3. **Hybrid RAG**: Structured legal reasoning required → Good for statutes/regulations
4. **Distributed Orchestration**: Very large documents → Best accuracy (Hebbia: 92%)

### Choose Hallucination Prevention:

1. **Citation Backing**: 10-15% improvement ✓ Easy
2. **RAG Grounding**: 17-34% error rate (large improvement from 68%)
3. **Multi-Model Ensemble**: 20-25% improvement but 3-5x cost
4. **Distributed Orchestration**: 24% improvement, 92% accuracy (best)
5. **Fine-Tuning**: 20-30% improvement, domain-specific
6. **Confidence Estimation**: 5-10% improvement, easy to add

### Best Practices Checklist:

- [ ] Custom legal embeddings (20B+ tokens minimum)
- [ ] Agentic architecture (iterative reasoning, not single-pass)
- [ ] Citation backing (every claim traceable)
- [ ] Multi-step planning (show users the strategy)
- [ ] Confidence indicators (tell users when uncertain)
- [ ] Knowledge graph validation (optional but recommended)
- [ ] Multi-model ensemble (for maximum accuracy)
- [ ] Jurisdiction filtering (restrict to applicable law)
- [ ] Performance benchmarking (Stanford hallucination test)

## COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGES FOR NEW ENTRANTS

### What's Working (Others Are Doing)
1. ✓ Agentic architecture (non-negotiable 2025+)
2. ✓ Custom legal embeddings (20B+ tokens)
3. ✓ Citation backing
4. ✓ Multi-model ensemble

### What's NOT Saturated (Differentiation Opportunities)
1. Vertical specialization (like EvenUp in personal injury)
2. International/jurisdictional focus (like Legora)
3. Regulations-as-code (like Norm AI)
4. Real-time data grounding (like Spellbook with market data)
5. Distributed orchestration for better accuracy
6. Hybrid human-AI (not fully autonomous)

### What's Oversaturated (Avoid)
1. General legal AI for research
2. Single-model approaches
3. RAG-only (without agentic reasoning)
4. Ignoring context window limits
5. Over-promising autonomy
