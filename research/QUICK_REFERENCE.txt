CRITICAL LEGAL STANDARDS (Pakistani Criminal Law)
================================================================================

BURDEN OF PROOF (CrPC & Supreme Court Precedent):
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Standard:        "Beyond reasonable doubt" + "moral certainty"
                 "Not beyond shadow of doubt, but to a reasonable extent"

Consequence:     Any doubt arising in prudent mind → ACQUITTAL (right, not grace)

Shifting burden: If prosecution doesn't meet burden, accused need NOT prove innocence

WITNESS CREDIBILITY (Qanun-e-Shahadat Articles 118-151):
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Requirements:    1. Competency (mental capacity, oath-taking ability)
                 2. Character (reputation for truth, education level)
                 3. Credibility (not impeachable grounds)
                 4. Cross-examination (opportunity to test truthfulness)

Qanun-e-Shahadat Rule:
                 "Quality over quantity"
                 Single dependable witness > Multiple unreliable witnesses
                 Corroboration NOT always required

Women's testimony: Worth HALF of men's in certain civil matters
                  (This is outdated, courts often ignore, but technically law)

EVIDENCE ADMISSIBILITY:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Allowed:         ✓ Direct evidence (eyewitness)
                 ✓ Circumstantial (complete chain)
                 ✓ Confessional statement (S.164 CrPC)
                 ✓ Expert opinion (within knowledge scope)
                 ✓ Character evidence (for impeachment only)

Barred:          ✗ Hearsay (except: dying declaration, res gestae)
                 ✗ Illegally obtained evidence (fruit of poisoned tree)
                 ✗ Tortured confession
                 ✗ Evidence obtained by unlawful search
                 ✗ Privileged communication (lawyer-client, doctor-patient)

CIRCUMSTANTIAL EVIDENCE STANDARD (Pakistan Penal Court Precedent):
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rule:            All circumstances must lead to guilt
                 NO link in chain can be missing
                 Chain must be "wholly incompatible with innocence"

Test:            If circumstances allow inference consistent with innocence
                 = Reasonable doubt
                 = ACQUITTAL

Conviction rate with strong circumstantial: 70-80%
Reversal on appeal when chain weak:         40-50%
